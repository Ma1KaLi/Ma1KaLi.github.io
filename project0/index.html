Part 1 — Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Two portraits taken with different shooting distances / focal lengths.

Why does stepping back + zooming look better?
When you shoot very close with a wide field-of-view, perspective exaggeration makes near features (like the nose) appear larger and far features (ears, sides of face) smaller. Stepping back changes the geometry (rays become more parallel), so relative size differences across the face shrink. Zooming then restores framing without re-introducing that close-up distortion. In short, position controls perspective; zoom controls framing.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Two urban/linear-perspective shots to compare “compressed” vs. “expanded” depth.

Why does the telephoto view look “flattened”?
From far away with a longer focal length, scene points subtend more similar angles, so distances along the street appear compressed—parallel lines seem more parallel, and layers stack closer together. Walking forward with a wider view increases perspective spread: near objects grow faster in the frame than far ones, so depth feels more pronounced.
